# EU AI Act Compliance Notes

This document maps current product controls to likely obligations under the EU AI Act for enterprise knowledge-assistant usage.

## Intended Use

- Internal enterprise retrieval and Q&A over customer-provided documents.
- Not intended for automated decision-making in employment, credit, law enforcement, or public services.

## Preliminary Risk Position

- Expected classification: limited-risk or minimal-risk assistant use cases (customer-specific legal review still required).

## Implemented Controls

- User authentication and role-based permissions
- Per-collection access controls
- Audit trails for mutations and admin actions
- Source citations in answers (traceability)
- Model/provider configurability for sovereignty deployments

## Transparency Controls

- Responses are generated by AI models and should be reviewed by users.
- Citations include source documents and page context.
- Missing policy details are explicitly signaled in prompts.

## Human Oversight

- Admin users can review logs, deactivate users/keys, and revoke access.
- Assistant outputs are advisory; customers retain final decision authority.

## Data Governance

- EU-only hosting runbook available (`docs/UPCLOUD_EU_DEPLOYMENT.md`)
- GDPR workflows documented (`docs/GDPR_COMPLIANCE.md`)
- On-prem profile for customers requiring full data isolation

## Technical Documentation Package (for customers)

- System architecture and deployment topology
- Model/provider configuration and fallback behavior
- Known limitations (hallucination risk, prompt injection risk)
- Monitoring and incident handling process
